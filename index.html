Research & Extracurriculars

Women in Computing at Cornell (WICC)

I was the Career Development Director for Women in Computing at Cornell (WICC). Having struggled through my first recruiting season and feeling overwhelmed, I created a course through WICC. I lectured weekly about recruitment topics, such as finding opportunities, interviewing resources, resume advice, and career paths.

The Lee Lab

Building off the breast cancer metastasis research I assisted with in high school, I conducted an independent research project to study the enhanced permeability and retention (EPR) effect in different types of breast cancer cells to improve drug delivery mechanisms. I performed nanoparticle drainage experiments on a biomimetic tumor-on-chip platform specifically curated by the Lee Lab. I was awarded a $5000 grant from Engineering Learning Iniatives for this project. Read more about the project here!

Cornell iGem

Cornell iGem is a project team that specializes in harnessing synthetic biology to solve real-world problems. As part of iGem, I helped create Collatrix, which focuses on developing protein-based hydrogel scaffolds for wound healing research and applications. Collatrix was developed by using collagen and gelatin to develop ECM-like hydrogels, and subsequently engineering E. Coli to produce Scl2 proteins to bind to and grow on the hydrogel structure. The project received a silver medal at the 2021 iGem Jamboree.

The Cardie Lab

The Cardie Lab was my introduction to using CS for large datasets. I assisted with a project studying Long Document Abstractive Summarization models. The project assessed different text summarization models such as BART and Longformer on different datasets and evaluated them using ROUGE metrics. My roles included dataset manipulation, serializing and deserializing data using python pickle for test, train, and validation datasets, and dataset representation in Jupyter Notebook.
